Churros

Ingredients

  • 1 cup water
  • 4 cups unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 Tbsp granulated sugar
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

For coating

  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 tsp ground cinnamon

Directions

  1. Whisk together both ingredients for coating in a shallow dish, set aside
  2. Heat about 1.5 inches of oil in a large pot over medium-high heat to 360 degrees Fahrenheit, prepare batter while oil is heating
  3. Add water, butter, sugar and salt to a large pan, bring to a boil over medium-high heat
  4. Add flout, reduce to medium-low heat and stir constantly until it gets a smooth, dough-like consistency (a few lumps are fine)
  5. Transfer to a large bowel or stand mixer, allow to sit until you can comfortable touch the bottom of the bowl, about 5 minutes
  6. Add vanilla and egg to the dough the begin blending immediately, continue blending until the mixture comes together again and is smooth
  7. Transfer to a piping bag fitted with a medium sized star tip
  8. Pipe mixture into the preheated oil, strips should be about 6 inches long, before cutting the end off the tip with clean scissors
  9. Fry about two minutes on each side, or until golden brown
  10. Transfer to a paper-towel lined container or plate, let drain for about 15 seconds
  11. Quickly toss in cinnamon-sugar mixture and roll or toss to coat
  12. Repeat until you've used all of the dough, frying no more than 5 churros at once

Garlic Salmon

Ingredients

  • 2 medium to large salmon fillets, skin on
  • 2 Tbsp pure olive oil (not virgin olive oil)
  • 3 garlic cloves, finely diced
  • 4 Tbsp unsalted butter

Directions

  1. Using a large, shallow pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat until barely popping
  2. Place both salmon fillets in the skillet, skin side up
  3. Make sure to constantly move the salmon to insure it doesn't stick to the pan
  4. After about 5 minutes, or once you can visibly see the meat grain on the side, flip the salmon to be skin side down
  5. Put in the butter and garlic
  6. For another approximately 5 minutes, or to desired crispiness, use a spoon to baste the hot butter over the salmon, slowly caramalizing the top
  7. Once crispy, remove from pan and allow to sit covered for about 10 minutes before serving

Strawberry Cheesecake

Ingredients

Biscuit base

  • 28 graham crackers
  • 8 Tbsp unsalted butter, melted

Cheesecake filling

  • 1 lb cream cheese, softened
  • 2 Tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1 1/2 cups caster sugar
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 3 eggs, room temperature

Strawberry topping

  • 1 lb strawberries, half diced and half halved
  • 2 Tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 tsp cornstarch
  • 2 Tbsp water

Directions

Preperation

  1. Preheat oven to 320 degrees Fehrenheit
  2. Line an 8" springform cake tin with butter and parchment paper, using the base upside down
  3. Butter and line the sides of the pan as well

Cheesecake biscuit base

  1. Break up crackers roughly by hand and place in a food processor (alternately, place in a ziploc bag and crush using a rolling pin)
  2. Blitz until fine crumbs. Add butter, briefly blitz until dispersed and resembles wet sand
  3. Pour into prepared cake tin, using a spatula to roughly spread over the base and up the walls
  4. Using a measuring cup or glass cup, compact and flatten the crumbs into the base and sides

Filling

  1. Use a mixer or bearer to beat the cream cheese until smooth, no longer than 20 seconds on speed 4(beating too long can cause the cheesecake to crack while baking)
  2. Add flour, beat for 5 seconds on speed 4 until just incorporated
  3. Add vanilla, sour cream, sugar, and lemon zest. Beat until just combibned (10 seconds max, speed 5)
  4. Add eggs one at a time, beat in between until just combined (5 seconds each), after the last one, beat until the egg is fully incorporated
  5. Pour into prepared crust
  6. Bake for 55 minutes. The top should be a very light golden brown, not cracked, and flat. It should jiggle slightly when you shake the pan.
  7. Cool the cake in the oven with the door slightly open, then transfer the whole pan into the fridge for four hours
  8. Remove sides, use overhanging paper to slide the cheesecake off the cake pan, then slide the cheesecake off the paper

Strawberry topping

  1. Placed chopped strawberries (1/4 lb), vanilla, sugar, and lemon juice in a sauce pan
  2. Simmer for 10 minutes, until strawberries breakdown
  3. Mix cornstarch with water, add into the saucepan and stir
  4. Add halved strawberries, cook 1 minute to soften
  5. Sauce should be syrupy - remove from stove and cool
  6. One cool, stir and adjust thickness with a small amount of water to get it to "oozing" consistency (be careful, don't add too much water at once!)
  7. Spoon onto cheesecake so it's covered in a single layer of strawberries, refrigerate another 2 hours
  8. Slice and serve with remaining sauce
